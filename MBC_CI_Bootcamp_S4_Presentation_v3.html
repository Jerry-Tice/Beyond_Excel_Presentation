D7 — Prevent Recurrence
Four components — all required. Reveal each one as you teach it.
Component 1
SOP & Work Instruction Updates
Update the procedure that governs the process where root cause was found. Name the document, owner, and completion date.
If there is no SOP — D7 means creating one.
Component 2
Training Implications
If the fix requires anyone to do something differently — they must be trained. Who, on what, and by when. An SOP nobody has seen doesn't prevent recurrence.
Component 3
PM & Maintenance Schedule Updates
If root cause involved equipment condition or maintenance frequency — update the PM schedule. Change it in the system, not on a sticky note.
Component 4
Systemic Prevention — Similar Processes at Risk
Where else does this vulnerability exist? Same equipment type? Same process step? Different facility? Identify them — then close them.
This list feeds Phase 2 scope directly.

D8 — Three Qualities of Effective Recognition
All three are required. Generic praise is not D8.
1
Specific
Names the people, the problem, and the measurable result. Not 'thank you for your hard work.' The result is part of the recognition.
'The Line 3 team reduced changeover downtime from 45 min/shift to under 10 — that is the standard for how we solve problems here.'
2
Sincere
Connects the result to real operational value. The leader demonstrates they understand what the team did and why it mattered — not scripted praise.
Reference the specific problem. Name the root cause they found. Connect it to production, quality, or safety outcomes.
3
Timely
Within one week of D6 validation confirmation. Not at the annual review. Not three months later. Recognition that arrives late loses its signal value.
Forum: team meeting, all-facility email, posted results board. The forum matters less than the visibility.

S6 Scoring Rubric
Standard: specificity and ownership — not completion
Dimension What Strong Looks Like What Fails
Problem Definition (D2) Observable condition. Is/Is Not complete. Impact quantified. Cause in problem statement. No numbers.
Root Cause Rigor (D4) Why 5 = systemic condition. Validation statement present. Why 5 is a person or behavior. No validation.
Corrective Action (D5) Eliminates D4 root cause. Alignment test answered. Addresses symptom not root cause.
Validation Plan (D6) Metric + baseline + threshold + named owner + honest status. No metric, no baseline, no owner.
Prevention Plan (D7) Named document + specific change + owner + date + status. 'We will update the SOPs' — no name, no date.
Recognition Plan (D8) Named people + what solved + impact + forum + date. 'Team will be recognized.' No names, no date.
